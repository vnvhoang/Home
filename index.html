I'm Hoang, a mechanical engineering graduate from Hanoi University of Science and Technology, located in the dynamic heart of Hanoi, Vietnam. My academic journey has focused on unraveling the intricacies of nanocomposite structures and their dynamic behaviors.

My expertise lies in investigating free vibration and transient responses exhibited by various forms of nanocomposite structures, spanning from plates to cylindrical and doubly curved shells. I delve deeply into materials such as Functionally Graded Materials (FGM) and functionally grade graphene nanoplatelets reinforced nanocomposites (FG-GPLRC), studying their composition and behavior within these structures.

My approach is rooted in various theoretical frameworks, including classical theory, the first-order shear deformation theory, and other higher-order theoretical models. Leveraging methodologies such as the Galerkin method, I meticulously explore and analyze factors influencing structural behavior.
